# Calculator Buttons Decoded: A Complete Guide for the Rest of Us

You have a calculator app. You see strange symbols: `sin`, `cos`, `log`, `e`, `!`, `π`, `^`, `RAD`, `INV`. You press them and numbers appear — but what do they *mean*?  

This post explains each one in plain English, with real‑world examples. No math degree required.

---

## 1. The Basics: +, -, ×, ÷ (you already know these)

These do exactly what you think. We’ll focus on the weird ones.

---

## 2. π (pi) – The circle number

**What it does:** π ≈ 3.14159. It’s the ratio of a circle’s circumference (distance around) to its diameter (distance across). Every circle has the same π.

**Real‑world use:**  
You have a round table 2 meters across. To find how much fabric for a tablecloth:  
Area = π × radius² = 3.14 × (1×1) ≈ 3.14 square meters.

---

## 3. ^ (caret) – Raise to a power

**What it does:** Multiply a number by itself a certain number of times.  
`3 ^ 4` = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 81.

**Real‑world use:**  
You invest $1,000 at 10% interest for 3 years.  
Final amount = 1000 × (1.10 ^ 3) = 1000 × 1.331 = $1,331.

---

## 4. ! (factorial) – Multiply all whole numbers down to 1

**What it does:** `5!` = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120.

**Real‑world use:**  
You have 3 books. How many ways to arrange them on a shelf?  
`3!` = 6 ways.

---

## 5. √ (square root) – The opposite of squaring

**What it does:** Finds the number that, when multiplied by itself, gives your number.  
√64 = 8 because 8×8=64.

**Real‑world use:**  
Your square garden has area 64 m². Side length = √64 = 8 m.

---

## 6. sin, cos, tan – Ladder math

Imagine a ladder leaning against a wall.

- **sin (sine)** = how high the ladder reaches (vertical height).  
- **cos (cosine)** = how far the ladder’s foot is from the wall (horizontal distance).  
- **tan (tangent)** = steepness = height ÷ distance.

**Example:** 10‑ft ladder at 30° angle.  
- Height = 10 × sin(30°) = 5 ft  
- Distance = 10 × cos(30°) ≈ 8.66 ft  
- Tan(30°) = 5 ÷ 8.66 ≈ 0.577 (for every 1 ft away, it goes up 0.577 ft)

**Real‑world use:**  
Roof pitch, wheelchair ramp steepness, leaning a ladder safely.

---

## 7. e (Euler’s number) – Continuous growth

**What it does:** e ≈ 2.71828. It appears whenever something grows smoothly without stopping (bacteria, interest, population).

**Real‑world use:**  
You have $1 at 100% continuous interest. After 1 year you have $e ≈ $2.72, not $2.

---

## 8. ln (natural log) – The undo button for e

**What it does:** ln answers “how many years of continuous growth to reach this number?”  
ln(2.71828) = 1 because e¹ = 2.71828.

**Real‑world use:**  
How long to double your money at 100% continuous interest?  
ln(2) ≈ 0.693 years (about 8 months).

---

## 9. log (base‑10 log) – Powers of 10

**What it does:** log answers “10 raised to what power gives this number?”  
log(100) = 2 because 10² = 100.

**Real‑world uses:**  
- **Earthquakes (Richter scale):** Magnitude 6 is 10× stronger than magnitude 5.  
- **Loudness (decibels):** 20 dB is 10× louder than 10 dB.  
- **pH (acidity):** pH 3 is 10× more acidic than pH 4.  
- **Digits in a number:** log(1000) = 3 → 1000 has 4 digits (3+1).

---

## 10. RAD (radian mode) – Measuring angles differently

**What it does:** Instead of degrees (0° to 360°), radian mode uses a circle’s radius to measure angles.  
360° = 2π radians ≈ 6.283.

**When to use it:**  
Higher math and physics usually use radians. For everyday problems (roof pitch, ladder angles), use DEG (degrees).

**How to switch:** Look for a MODE button. If you see RAD on screen, press MODE and choose DEG.

---

## 11. INV (inverse) – The undo button

**What it does:** Reverses the last function.  
- `sin` → `INV` + `sin` gives the angle from a sine value.  
- `log` → `INV` + `log` gives 10ˣ.  
- `x²` → `INV` + `x²` gives √x.

**Example:** You know sin(θ) = 0.5. What’s θ?  
Type `0.5` → `INV` → `sin` → 30°.

---

## 12. Putting it all together – A quick cheat sheet

| Button | What it does | Example |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| `π`    | Circle constant (3.14) | Area = πr² |
| `^`    | Power | 2^3 = 8 |
| `!`    | Factorial | 4! = 24 |
| `√`    | Square root | √81 = 9 |
| `sin`  | Height from angle | 10×sin(30°)=5 ft |
| `cos`  | Distance from angle | 10×cos(30°)=8.66 ft |
| `tan`  | Steepness (height/distance) | tan(30°)=0.577 |
| `e`    | Natural growth base (2.718) | e^1 = 2.718 |
| `ln`   | Natural log (undo e) | ln(2.718)=1 |
| `log`  | Base‑10 log (undo 10ˣ) | log(100)=2 |
| `RAD`  | Angle mode (radians) | 180° = π rad |
| `INV`  | Inverse (undo) | INV+sin gives angle |
