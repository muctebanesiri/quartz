# Weather App Symbols Decoded: A Complete Guide for the Rest of Us

You open your weather app. You see a sun, a cloud, a weird curved arrow, and numbers like “1013 hPa” or “UV 5”. What does it all mean?

This post explains each one in plain English, with real‑world examples. No meteorology degree required.

---

## 1. The Temperature (°C / °F) – How hot or cold

**What it means:** The warmth of the air.  
**Real‑world use:** If it says 30°C (86°F), wear shorts. If –5°C (23°F), wear a coat.

---

## 2. Feels Like (or “RealFeel”) – What your body feels

**What it means:** Combines temperature + wind + humidity. Wind makes cold feel colder; humidity makes hot feel hotter.

**Example:** Actual temp = 30°C, but humidity 80% → Feels like 38°C (sticky!).  
Actual temp = 5°C, wind 30 km/h → Feels like 1°C.

---

## 3. Rain / Precipitation (%) – Chance of getting wet

**What it means:** Probability that at least 0.01 inch (0.25 mm) of rain will fall anywhere in the forecast area during the time window.

**Example:** 30% chance of rain = out of 10 similar days, it rained on 3. Not “30% of the area will get rain”.

**Real‑world use:** 60% or higher → grab an umbrella. 20% → probably fine.

---

## 4. Rain amount (mm or inches) – How much water

**What it means:** Depth of water if it all stayed where it fell.

**Example:** 10 mm rain = about 1 cm of water on the ground.  
Heavy rain = 25+ mm per hour (flash flood risk).

---

## 5. Cloud cover (☁️ symbols) – How much sky is hidden

- **☀️ Clear / Sunny:** 0–10% clouds  
- **⛅ Partly cloudy:** 25–50% clouds  
- **☁️ Mostly cloudy:** 75–90% clouds  
- **☁️☁️ Overcast:** 100% clouds (grey sky)

**Real‑world use:** Partly cloudy = good for photos. Overcast = maybe indoor plans.

---

## 6. Wind speed & direction (arrow + number)

**What it means:**  
- **Speed:** how fast air moves (km/h, mph, or knots).  
- **Direction:** where the wind comes *from* (not where it’s going).

**Example:** An arrow pointing down (↓) with “15 km/h” means wind blowing **from north to south** at 15 km/h.

**Real‑world use:**  
- 0–10 km/h: calm (good for barbecue)  
- 20–40 km/h: breezy (hat might fly)  
- 50+ km/h: strong (dangerous for driving high vehicles)

---

## 7. Wind gust – Sudden bursts

**What it means:** A short spike in wind speed, often 20–50% higher than the average wind.

**Example:** Wind 30 km/h, gusts 50 km/h → hold your umbrella tight.

---

## 8. Humidity (%) – How much moisture in the air

**What it means:** Percentage of water vapor the air can hold at current temperature. 100% = air is full (rain or fog likely).

**Real‑world use:**  
- 30–50%: comfortable  
- 70%+ with heat: feels sticky, sweat doesn’t evaporate  
- 90%+ with cold: feels bone‑chilling damp

---

## 9. Air pressure (hPa, mbar, inHg) – Weight of the air

**What it means:** Atmospheric pressure.  
- **High pressure (1020+ hPa)** → clear skies, calm weather.  
- **Low pressure (990–1010 hPa)** → clouds, rain, storms.  
- **Rapidly dropping pressure** → storm coming soon.

**Real‑world use:** If pressure drops 5+ hPa in 3 hours, expect bad weather within 12–24 hours.

---

## 10. UV index (1–11+) – Sunburn risk

| UV | Risk | Time to burn (fair skin) |
|----|------|--------------------------|
| 0–2 | Low | 60+ minutes |
| 3–5 | Moderate | 30–45 minutes |
| 6–7 | High | 15–25 minutes |
| 8–10 | Very high | 10 minutes |
| 11+ | Extreme | 5 minutes |

**Real‑world use:** UV 6+ → wear sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses.

---

## 11. Visibility (km or miles) – How far you can see

**What it means:** Greatest distance at which you can identify a dark object against the horizon.

**Real‑world use:**  
- 10+ km: clear  
- 1–5 km: haze or light fog (drive carefully)  
- <1 km: dense fog (use low beams, slow down)

---

## 12. Sunrise / Sunset – Day length

**What it means:** When the sun’s upper edge appears or disappears.

**Real‑world use:** If sunset is 5:00 PM and you’re hiking, bring a flashlight.

---

## 13. Moon phases (🌑🌓🌕🌗) – Tides and night light

- 🌑 New moon – dark night (good for stargazing)  
- 🌕 Full moon – bright night (fishing, nighttime walks)  
- 🌗 Last quarter – half moon

**Real‑world use:** Full moon = higher tides (beachgoers & boats be aware).

---

## 14. Thunderstorm / Lightning icon (⚡)

**What it means:** Thunderstorms expected. Lightning, heavy rain, possibly hail or gusty winds.

**Real‑world use:** Avoid open fields, tall trees, water, and metal objects. Stay indoors until 30 minutes after last thunder.

---

## 15. Fog icon (☁️ with lines or “mist”)

**What it means:** Tiny water droplets suspended near ground, reducing visibility.

**Real‑world use:** Turn on low‑beam headlights, not high beams (high beams reflect back and blind you). Increase following distance.

---

## 16. Snow icon (❄️) and snow amount

**What it means:** Frozen precipitation.  
- Light snow: flurries, little accumulation.  
- Heavy snow: 10+ cm (4+ inches) can disrupt travel.

**Real‑world use:** Snow amount × 10 ≈ water content (e.g., 10 cm snow = about 1 cm water).

---

## 17. Freezing rain / Sleet – Slippery danger

**What it means:**  
- **Freezing rain:** rain that freezes on contact with cold surfaces → black ice.  
- **Sleet:** ice pellets that bounce.

**Real‑world use:** If freezing rain is forecast, don’t drive unless necessary. Power lines and trees can snap.

---

## 18. Radar map (green/yellow/red)

- **Green:** light rain  
- **Yellow:** moderate rain  
- **Orange/Red:** heavy rain or storms  
- **Purple/White:** hail or very intense rain

**Real‑world use:** Red moving toward you → expect downpour in 10–20 minutes.

---

## 19. Hourly vs. daily forecast

- **Hourly:** what to expect in the next 24–48 hours (good for planning a few hours ahead).  
- **Daily:** high/low temps, overall conditions (good for planning a picnic or trip tomorrow).

---

## 20. Weather alerts (⚠️, 🟡, 🟠, 🔴)

| Color | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 🟡 Yellow | Be aware | Check updates |
| 🟠 Orange | Be prepared | Consider changing plans |
| 🔴 Red | Take action | Dangerous weather imminent – follow official advice |

Examples: thunderstorm warning, heat wave, blizzard, flood.

---

## Quick cheat sheet

| Symbol / Term | What it means |
|---------------|----------------|
| 30% rain | 30% chance of measurable rain |
| 💧 5 mm | 5 millimeters of water |
| ☁️ Cloudy | Mostly overcast |
| 🌬️ 15 km/h ↓ | Wind from north at 15 km/h |
| 💧 80% humidity | Air feels muggy |
| 1013 hPa | Normal air pressure |
| UV 7 | High sunburn risk |
| 2 km visibility | Foggy – drive slow |
| 🌕 | Full moon (bright night) |
| ⚡ | Thunderstorm – go inside |

---

## Final tip: Use the “feels like” and radar most often

- Check **feels like** to dress right.  
- Check **radar** before stepping out for 30+ minutes.  
- Check **UV** before a beach or hiking day.

Weather apps are powerful – but only if you understand them. Now you do.
